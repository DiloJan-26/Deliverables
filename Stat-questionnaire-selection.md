# Best 3 questionnaire topics for your project

## **Rank 1 — Perceived Stress Scale, PSS**

### Recommended project title

**Adaptation and Validation of the Perceived Stress Scale for Measuring Academic Stress among Postgraduate Data Science and AI Students**

### Why this is the best choice

This is my **strongest recommendation** for your group.

The project PDF itself gives **Perceived Stress Scale** as one of the main examples and describes it as a scale for measuring perceived stress over the past month. It is also listed as one of the widely used stress scales. 

This suits your programme very well because Data Science and AI master’s students often face:

* coding assignments
* statistics and mathematics pressure
* machine learning project deadlines
* research work
* group projects
* balancing job and studies
* pressure to learn new AI tools
* final exams and presentations

So you can adapt the PSS into your context without making it artificial.

### Why it is easy to do

PSS-10 has only **10 main items**, so respondents will complete it quickly. That makes it suitable for Google Forms.

It is also easy to analyze using:

* Cronbach’s Alpha
* descriptive statistics
* t-test
* ANOVA
* correlation
* regression
* confidence intervals

### Possible research questions

1. What is the level of perceived academic stress among postgraduate Data Science and AI students?
2. Is there a significant difference in stress between working and non-working students?
3. Does weekly study workload significantly predict perceived stress?
4. Is sleep duration associated with perceived stress?

### Possible hypotheses

**H0:** There is no significant difference in perceived stress between working and non-working postgraduate students.
**H1:** There is a significant difference in perceived stress between working and non-working postgraduate students.

Or:

**H0:** Weekly study hours do not significantly predict perceived stress.
**H1:** Weekly study hours significantly predict perceived stress.

### Why this can look impressive

Even though it is simple, it can become strong if you connect it to the **Data Science and AI learning environment**.

For example:

> Academic stress among postgraduate Data Science and AI students may be influenced by technical workload, programming assignments, research deadlines, mathematical difficulty, and the pressure to keep up with rapidly changing AI technologies.

That gives your paper a modern and relevant angle.

### Final judgment

**Best overall choice.**
It is meaningful, manageable, researchable, and directly suitable for Statistical Inference.

# Rank 2 — Motivated Strategies for Learning Questionnaire, MSLQ

### Recommended project title

**Adaptation and Validation of the Motivated Strategies for Learning Questionnaire for AI-Assisted Learning among Postgraduate Data Science and AI Students**

### Why this is a strong choice

The MSLQ measures **motivation and learning strategies** and was developed for higher education settings, according to the project list. 

This is highly related to your degree because Data Science and AI students need strong self-directed learning skills. They often learn through:

* online materials
* coding practice
* AI tools
* research papers
* GitHub projects
* machine learning experiments
* self-study outside lectures

This topic sounds very academic and conference-friendly.

### Why it is impressive

This topic connects directly with modern education and AI.

You can explore how postgraduate students use learning strategies in a technical AI-related degree.

Example research angle:

> In Data Science and AI education, students must combine statistical reasoning, programming, mathematical modeling, and independent research. Therefore, motivation and learning strategies are essential for academic success.

### Possible research questions

1. What motivational and learning strategy patterns are seen among postgraduate Data Science and AI students?
2. Do working students and full-time students differ in learning strategies?
3. Does self-regulated learning predict academic confidence?
4. Are AI-tool usage and learning strategies associated?

### Possible hypotheses

**H0:** There is no significant difference in learning strategy scores between students who frequently use AI tools and students who do not.
**H1:** There is a significant difference in learning strategy scores between students who frequently use AI tools and students who do not.

### Difficulty level

This is more advanced than PSS.

The issue is that the full MSLQ can be long. Some versions have many items, so students may not complete the Google Form carefully. You may need to select validated subscales rather than use the full questionnaire.

### Final judgment

**Most impressive academic choice**, especially if your group wants a Data Science/AI education angle.
But it is more difficult than PSS.

# Rank 3 — Academic Motivation Scale, AMS

### Recommended project title

**Adaptation and Validation of the Academic Motivation Scale for Postgraduate Data Science and AI Students**

### Why this is a good choice

AMS measures **intrinsic and extrinsic motivation** and is based on Self-Determination Theory, according to the list. 

This is also suitable for your programme because students may be motivated by different reasons:

* interest in AI
* career growth
* salary expectations
* pressure from employers
* academic achievement
* research interest
* desire to work in data science
* fear of falling behind in the AI field

This gives you a clean and meaningful research direction.

### Possible research questions

1. What type of motivation is dominant among postgraduate Data Science and AI students?
2. Is motivation different between employed and unemployed students?
3. Does motivation predict study engagement?
4. Is motivation associated with confidence in Data Science and AI skills?

### Possible hypotheses

**H0:** There is no significant difference in academic motivation between employed and non-employed postgraduate students.
**H1:** There is a significant difference in academic motivation between employed and non-employed postgraduate students.

### Why it is easier than MSLQ

AMS is usually more focused than MSLQ. It deals mainly with motivation, while MSLQ covers both motivation and learning strategies. So AMS is easier to explain and analyze.

### Weakness

It may be slightly less directly connected to “stress,” while your project PDF gives stress-related examples strongly. So you may need to justify why academic motivation is suitable from the appendix list.

### Final judgment

**Good and manageable choice.**
It is easier than MSLQ but slightly less powerful than PSS for this specific assignment.
